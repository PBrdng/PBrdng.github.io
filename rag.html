Random Algebraic Geometry


This is the website for the lecture Random Algebraic Geometry in the winter semester 2021/2022 at the University of Leipzig.

Location

The lecture will take place every tuesday from 11:15-12:45 in room Seminargebäude (SG) 2-14 of the University of Leipzig.
The lecture will start on October 12, 2021.

  

Description

This course will deal with the basic problem of understanding the structure (e.g. the geometry and topology) of the set of solutions of real polynomial equations with random coefficients. The simplest case of interest is the count of the number of real zeroes of a random univariate polynomial whose coefficients are Gaussian random variables – this problem was pioneered by Kac in the 1940s. More generally algebraic geometers might be interested, for example, in the number of components of a random real plane curve, or in the expected number of real solutions of more advanced counting problems. I will present the basic techniques for attacking this type of questions, trying to emphasize the connections of classical algebraic geometry with random matrix theory and random ﬁelds.

Tentative schedule

1. Basic definitions. Gaussian distribution. Kostlan distribution. Examples of generic and expected properties
2. Riemannian manifolds. Riemannian volume. Probability distributions on Riemannian manifolds. The coarea formula. Isometries and Riemannian submersions.
3. Proof of the coarea formula. Volume of the orthogonal group.
4. Volume of Riemannian homogeneous spaces, of the sphere and projective space, of the Grassmannian and of the Segre and Veronese variety.
5. Random maps. One-dimensional Kac-Rice formula. Root density of Kac-Polynomials
6. The Kac-Rice formula on manifolds. Root density of Kostlan polynomials.
7. The Kac-Rice formula for vector bundles. Lines on a cubic surface. The Fary-Milnor theorem.
8. Invariant measures on the space of polynomials.
9. Integral geometry in projective space. Volume and degree of projective varieties. The probabilistic Bézout theorem.
10. Integral geometry in Riemannian homogeneous spaces.
11. Random Matrices. Random tensors. Distribution of Eigenvalues. Segre, Veronese and Grassmannian revisited.
12. Topology of algebraic hypersurfaces. The small degree approximation theorem.
